Volunteer Opportunities

Presidential Volunteer Service Award

The Presidential Volunteer Service Award (PVSA), established in 2003, honors individuals, families, and groups for their dedication to volunteerism and civic engagement. U.S. citizens or permanent residents can earn bronze, silver, or gold awards based on the hours of service completed in a year. Recipients receive a medal, certificate, and a letter from the President in recognition of their contributions.

Volunteer Opportunities

Presidential Volunteer Service Award

Engaging youth in local projects promotes responsibility and compassion, helping to develop future leaders. Involving young people in volunteer activities fosters accountability and empathy, benefiting both the community and shaping them into active citizens committed to service.

REF is able to verify and provide volunteer hours eligible for the President's Volunteer Service Award. We are also committed to supporting additional community service initiatives. Individuals interested in collaborating on projects or volunteering with REF are encouraged to reach out to us for more information.

Volunteering Opportunities

Possible Project List:

Environmental Clean-up Projects:
- Organize beach, park, or neighborhood clean-ups.
- Lead recycling or tree-planting initiatives in your local area.
Youth Mentorship Programs:
- Start or join a tutoring program for local students.
- Create workshops on life skills or career readiness for teens.
Community Food Drives:
- Partner with local food banks to organize food donation drives.
- Set up a community garden to provide fresh produce for food-insecure families.
Elderly Assistance:
- Develop a senior outreach program to help with chores, shopping, or companionship.
- Organize virtual or in-person classes for seniors on technology use.
Homeless Outreach Initiatives:
- Lead a project to distribute care packages to homeless shelters.
- Organize a drive to collect clothes, blankets, and hygiene supplies for homeless individuals.
Animal Shelter Support:
- Volunteer at local animal shelters by helping with cleaning, feeding, or walking animals.
- Run a community campaign to collect supplies or raise funds for animal shelters.
Health and Wellness Campaigns:
- Organize a community fitness challenge or health education event.
- Partner with local clinics to offer free health screenings or flu shot clinics.
Arts and Culture Programs:
- Host free art classes or creative workshops for children in neighborhoods.
- Lead a public mural or beautification project in your community.
Cultural Diversity and Inclusion Mental Health Awareness:
- Organize educational events that celebrate different cultures, promoting diversity and inclusion.
- Set up discussion groups to address and raise awareness about social justice issues.
Educational Resources Development:
- Create a free community library or book exchange program.
- Design and distribute educational kits for children in communities.
